Why this petition exists

This petition exists to build a verifiable, public record of how many Canadians have lost confidence in the current federal government. Every verified signature serves as a direct democratic signal — one that cannot be dismissed as a poll or opinion piece. The goal is to demonstrate, in hard numbers, the depth of distrust Canadians hold toward the sitting government, and to use that public record to apply legitimate pressure on both the governing party and the Governor General to consider whether confidence in the House can be maintained. Under Canada's Westminster system, the Governor General retains the constitutional authority to respond to a clear and documented breakdown of public trust.

Grounds for Non-Confidence

Concerns that have prompted this petition, drawn from public record and parliamentary proceedings:

Conflict of interest

Personal holdings & the Canada Strong Fund

Concerns that the Prime Minister retains significant corporate holdings while the government seeds a $25 billion fund in overlapping sectors, despite Ethics Committee guidance to fully divest.

Democratic process

Minority mandate & floor-crossing

Concerns that a 2025 minority result reflects voters deliberately limiting power, and that cross-floor moves are being used to manufacture an effective majority without a renewed mandate.

Transparency

Bill C-25 & fundraising disclosure rollbacks

Concerns that Bill C-25 weakens 2018 fundraising disclosure laws, reducing accountability for political financing and reopening the door to closed-door, undisclosed political events.

Human rights

Uyghur genocide recognition & trade policy

Concerns that recent trade and departmental policy decisions may contradict Canada's 2021 unanimous parliamentary recognition of the Uyghur genocide.
